Bestiary: Book of Beasts — Structured Overview

Segmented into uniform cards for lower cognitive load and predictable reading rhythm.

A — Orientation

📖 Overview

What is a Bestiary?

Image suggestion: Griffin detail, Book of Flowers.

  • Medieval “book of beasts” (real + mythical), c. 500–1500.
  • Pairs animal descriptions with Christian meanings; devotional + entertaining.
  • Stories spread into tapestries, sculpture, and later culture.

🏛️ Exhibition

The First Major Showcase

  • Book of Beasts assembled 100+ works, ~⅓ of surviving illuminated bestiaries.
  • Frames key stories, typical contents, and the tradition’s influence.

🧭 How Entries Work

Text + Image + Order

  • Each entry: short text + iconic image.
  • Common order: land → birds → serpents → sea.
  • From church instruction to elite devotion and entertainment.

B — Symbol Stories

🦄 Unicorn

Tamed by a Maiden

Image: Ashmole Bestiary unicorn.

  • Lore: wild, rests in a maiden’s lap; hunters seize it.
  • Meaning: Incarnation—unicorn = Christ; maiden = Mary; wounding = Passion.
  • Later becomes a courtly-love emblem beyond manuscripts.

🪶 Object Story

Parade Saddle

  • Carved bone saddle shows unicorn + maiden—story leaves the page.

🦁 Lion

King of Beasts

  • Often first entry; cubs “revived” on day three → Resurrection type.
  • Pages stack scenes (adult, cubs, revival) for quick reading.

C — The Four Orders of Animals

🐾 Land

Parandrus

  • Moves horns independently to face two threats.
  • Artist shows a horn “cutting” the text—a playful text–image interaction.

🕊️ Birds

Eagle

  • Chief of birds; sight renewed by gazing at the sun → spiritual renewal.

🐉 Serpents

Dragon

  • King of serpents; diagonal body dominates page.
  • Tail interrupts text to underscore power.

🌊 Sea

Whale as Island

  • Sailors camp on its back; fire triggers a dive—warning against deception.
  • Art captures the instant of realization (shock moment).

D — Beyond the Book

🏺 Object

Elephant Candlestick

  • Elephant = strength; howdah = military might.
  • Design integrates candle spike and wax-catching rim.

🦊 Literature

Reynard the Fox

  • Trickster feigns death; becomes a popular literary cycle.
  • French word for fox (renard) stems from these tales.

🧵 Tapestry

Millefleurs Trinity

  • Stag, unicorn, lion aligned vertically—read as the Trinity.

📜 Cross-Tradition

Rothschild Pentateuch Lion

  • Lion reviving cub paired with Genesis/Abraham & Isaac.
  • Shows allegory across religions and contexts.

E — Knowledge & Maps

📚 Encyclopedias

Bestiary → Natural History

  • 1100–1300 encyclopedias absorb animal lore, often minus overt symbolism.

🗺️ Cartography

Creatures on Maps

  • Animals mark regions as exotic/dangerous as travel expands.

🦅🦁 Hybrid

The Griffin & “Relics”

  • Featured in early encyclopedias; said to carry off adults.
  • “Griffin claws” were often ibex horns; “eggs” were ostrich eggs.

F — Modern Echoes

🎨 Revival

Apollinaire & Dufy

  • 20th-century bestiaries pair text + bold woodcuts, echoing block books.

🧬 Contemporary

Kate Clark Hybrids

  • Human–animal sculptures probe identity, instinct, and ethics.
  • Modern echo of moral inquiry once carried by medieval beasts.

Tip: On mobile, these bands stack in the same order to preserve continuity.
